# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are the site owner and readers who browse long-form posts, diary archives, work logs, and imported Gcores talk fragments over time. They usually arrive to read, revisit, search, or follow a chronological trail rather than complete a transactional workflow.

## Product Purpose

This site is a durable personal archive built with Zola. It exists to keep writing, notes, work logs, and social fragments easy to publish, preserve, and browse years later. Success means the content remains stable, readable, linkable, and maintainable without depending on a fragile application stack.

## Brand Personality

Plain, archival, and slightly mechanical. The voice should feel like a maintained terminal notebook: direct, personal, text-first, and comfortable with long records.

## Anti-references

Do not turn the site into a SaaS-style marketing page, a card-heavy blog template, a social-feed clone, or a decorative magazine layout. Avoid glossy gradients, glass panels, oversized rounded cards, ornamental hero sections, and UI that competes with the writing.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve the archive first: URLs, dates, and content structure must stay stable enough for old entries to keep meaning.
2. Make long reading quiet: typography, spacing, and navigation should reduce effort without adding visual performance.
3. Keep the system repairable: prefer static generation, small templates, and plain Markdown over runtime-heavy behavior.
4. Let chronology lead: diary and talk surfaces should make date order, year boundaries, and backtracking obvious.
5. Add interaction only where it helps browsing: lazy loading, pagination, and outline controls are acceptable when they improve continuity without hiding content from non-JS readers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA readability for body text and controls. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic headings, reduced-motion behavior, and content access when JavaScript fails. Mobile reading should not require horizontal scrolling or tiny tap targets.
