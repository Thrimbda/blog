# Design Context

## Design North Star

Terminal Paper Archive: warm paper, dark ink, narrow reading columns, ASCII identity, thin rules, and text-first controls.

## Visual Language

- Background and surfaces use the existing paper/ink palette.
- Lists stay as rows and ruled text, not cards.
- Controls are textual or bracketed where possible.
- Metadata remains quiet and subordinate to entry content.
- Wide screens preserve readable measures rather than stretching the archive.

## Typography

- Existing monospace/serif fallback identity is preserved.
- Body text stays compact but readable.
- Dates and metadata use tabular, quiet treatment.
- Large marketing-style hero type is out of scope.

## Interaction

- Archive browsing starts as ordinary static pagination.
- Infinite loading enhances the existing next page link.
- Date access uses native, touch-friendly form controls.
- Mobile should expose a clear path to a specific date without forcing readers through a huge table of contents.

## Responsive Rules

- Mobile: single column, sticky date access, full-width touch controls, collapsed nonessential chrome.
- Laptop: left archive tools plus readable feed.
- Wide desktop: constrained content, sticky archive tools, no over-stretched lines.

## Prohibited Moves

- Do not convert daily entries into cards.
- Do not add decorative gradients, badges, heavy panels, or ornamental motion.
- Do not make daily browsing depend on a client-rendered app.
